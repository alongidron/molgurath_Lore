# Isyra

**Age:** Unknown    
**Size:** 1.80     
**Magic:** ice, fire, lightning     
**Source of Magic:** Isyra bound her life to her castle, turning it into a living mana pool. As long as the castle stands, her mana will never run out. If she dies, the castle is destroyed with her.
She also draws power from her bonded companions, gaining strength through her pets as well. 
---
# Lore
From a young age, Isyra was a formidable warrior. She was raised in battle and fell in love with combat early in her life. As she grew older, she joined the Witch Queen’s army and quickly rose through its ranks. Her talent, discipline, and ferocity soon earned her the title of general—one of the queen’s most trusted commanders.

But her world changed when the Witch Queen died. Her successor, Queen Khalyra, ruled with a far more peaceful philosophy. Isyra despised this new approach. To her, strength was forged through conflict, not restraint. Unable to accept the new order, she abandoned the kingdom to carve out one of her own.

Having mastered ice magic, her instincts drew her northward. Yet she soon realized the truth of that frozen land—the north was a merciless place, where survival itself was a constant battle.

To survive, Isyra was forced to master her two remaining magical disciplines—lightning and fire. Through hardship and relentless struggle, she tempered these powers until they answered her will as readily as ice. In time, she forged a kingdom of her own, not of witches, but of the brutal northern creatures who chose to follow her strength and leadership.

Eventually, her rise did not go unnoticed. Velmora herself sought Isyra out, impressed by what she had built in such a merciless land. Recognizing her as a ruler worthy of standing among legends, Velmora invited Isyra to join the Overlord Alliance.

Isyra was summoned to her first Overlord meeting, where she was stunned to see Velmora standing beside Khalyra. Until that moment, Isyra had believed Velmora to be the representative of the witches.

When the meeting concluded, unresolved resentment surfaced. Isyra challenged Khalyra to a duel. Khalyra accepted without hesitation—and emerged victorious.

The outcome left Isyra shaken. She could not understand how a witch who wielded only a single form of magic had defeated one gifted with three. Sensing her confusion, Velmora revealed the truth and explained everything Isyra had failed to see. She urged Isyra to release her anger, warning her that pride and grudges would only weaken her.

Isyra’s life entered a golden age. With each passing day she grew stronger, and her kingdom continued to expand under her rule.

Then came the fateful summons.

An angel called all the Overlords together and convinced them to join his cause—to wage war against Hell itself. The Overlords agreed. Isyra was assigned a crucial task: to hunt down the Knights of Aool and weaken the Demon King before the final confrontation.

Isyra succeeded. One by one, the Knights of Aool fell before her. As she finished her task, she saw the angelic host meant to face Aool advancing toward him. She knew she was not meant to join that battle.

But she chose to fight anyway.

When she entered the conflict, she realized the terrible truth too late. The death of his knights had not weakened Aool—it had strengthened him, feeding his power and fury.

Isyra fought regardless.

And in that battle against the Demon King, she perished.

Yet death did not claim her soul.

Before Aool could seize it, Isyra’s soul was torn away and cast into her Life Trial—a brutal crucible where she was forced to fight endlessly for survival. Time lost meaning. Memories blurred. Purpose faded. Eventually, she no longer remembered why she fought, only that she must.

When the trial finally ended, Isyra awoke reborn—her soul bound within the body of a newborn royal witch. Her past life was buried deep within her, dormant and forgotten… waiting for the day it would rise again.

In this new life, Isyra was raised within a loving family—something she had never known before. Yet despite that warmth, jealousy took root in her heart. Her sister, Elyxara, received far more of their mother Luminara’s attention, and Isyra felt constantly overshadowed.

Desperate to be seen, to prove herself worthy of that love, Isyra sought to accomplish something extraordinary. As a fire witch, she believed her path was obvious. Convinced that flame would triumph over frost, she set her sights on defeating an ice creature—certain it would be an easy victory.

But Isyra chose the wrong fight.

Seeking glory, she accepted a challenge far beyond her strength. She confronted a cursed ice giant—believing her fire magic would be enough. It was not. The creature’s power overwhelmed her, and she would have died had fate not intervened.

Elyxara had followed her. Realizing the danger, she called for their mother. Together, Elyxara and Luminara managed to defeat the ice giant—but victory came at a terrible cost. The battle carried a curse that bound itself to both Isyra and Luminara. Slowly, relentlessly, their bodies began to freeze from within, inching toward death.

In those final moments, Luminara understood the truth. She saw why Isyra had acted—not out of arrogance, but out of longing to be seen and loved.

Choosing her daughter over herself, Luminara made a final decision. She told Elyxara to give Isyra the ferret necklace and to forgive her for her reckless choice.

With trembling hands, Elyxara placed the necklace around Isyra’s neck. Before her eyes, the ferret charm came to life, radiating warmth. As it touched Isyra, the ice receded—and her memories returned.

She remembered everything.

She remembered her past life, her fall, her trial. She remembered that the ferret had been a gift from Velmora long ago.

And more than anything, she was stunned that it had survived—even after her death.

As Isyra’s memories returned, Elyxara felt it immediately—something was wrong. Before she could react or speak, Isyra vanished without a word.

Isyra first returned to her castle, only to find it in ruins. What she had built in her former life was gone, reduced to echoes and stone. Seeking answers, she traveled north to the Ice Lord’s domain, but he too had disappeared, his presence erased as if he had never ruled.

With dread growing in her chest, Isyra went to Velmora. There, she finally learned the truth. Velmora told her everything that had occurred after her death—the wars, the losses, and the price paid by those she had once stood beside.

The revelations shattered her. Rage and grief consumed her, especially the knowledge that she had not been there to save her mother—or to prevent all that followed. Unable to remain, Isyra left at once.

She returned briefly to Elyxara and told her what had happened, what she had learned, and what she now remembered. Then, without hesitation, Isyra departed once more—this time with purpose.

She set out to rebuild her kingdom, determined to reclaim what had been lost and forge something stronger from the ashes of her past.

To rebuild her kingdom, Isyra first needed to reclaim her strength.

She began by reforging her tools. Pouring countless defensive spells into her hat, she bound it to her staff, merging the two into a single weapon—a new staff shaped like an umbrella, both shield and focus, born from survival rather than elegance.

Seeking greater power, Isyra traveled to Hell, intending to tame a fire giant. Instead, she encountered something far more dangerous: the Lava Golem King. Against all expectations, she defeated and bound the colossal being, claiming its strength as her own.

No longer reliant on the ferret’s warmth to keep her alive, Isyra turned inward. She began to experiment with her frozen curse, refusing to see it as a weakness. Through relentless practice, she learned to reshape the curse itself into magic. The price was steep—her fire magic faded, replaced entirely by ice.

But this was not a loss.

It was exactly what she wanted. With her new mastery over ice, Isyra rebuilt her castle from frost and will alone, stronger than it had ever been before.

Only one thing remained to reclaim her former power. Lightning.

To regain it, Isyra journeyed far and wide in search of a legendary creature—Zephyron Vael, the Storm Sovereign, a colossal sky-born beast feared across realms as the King of the Storms. To face it would mean risking everything once more.

Although Zephyron commanded both wind and lightning, Isyra chose to take only his lightning magic, leaving the rest untouched. With her power nearly restored, she pushed herself into brutal training, testing how long she could survive without warmth. She began with thirty seconds. Over time, she endured minutes… then hours… until she could withstand the cold for half an hour unaided.

From time to time, she allowed herself brief breaks—often to help Elyxara care for her children—but beyond that, Isyra remained distant from the events that unfolded across the realms. Years passed in near silence.

Then one day, Velmora came to her. She revealed her intention to free the Ice Lord and warned Isyra that his mind might not survive his imprisonment. She would need Isyra’s help if he lost himself.

Isyra agreed.

When the Ice Lord was finally freed, it was Isyra who stood beside him, guiding him back to himself before madness could take hold. Together, they rebuilt his frozen kingdom, restoring what had been broken by time and loss.

And through this act—through strength, patience, and resolve—Isyra reclaimed everything she had once lost.
