# bell

**Age:** Unknown    
**Size:** 1.68     
**Magic:** gravity

**Source of Magic:** The first Bell King found the heart of a dead god.

Instead of consuming it, he forged it into the first Royal Bell.

Every royal descendant carries a fragment of that heart.

Gravity is simply the remnant of the god's authority. 
# Lore

Bell was born into the royal family of the Bell Kingdom, one of the oldest Overlord bloodlines in existence. For generations, the Bell royalty had a single obsession: war.

The bells possessed exceptionally long lifespans, yet they spent those centuries fighting endless conflicts. Kings and queens marched to battle without hesitation, seeking glory, territory, and honor. Each generation inherited the grudges of the last, and the wars never truly ended.

Bell's parents were among the many rulers who died pursuing those ambitions.

Still young when he inherited the throne, Bell found himself ruling a kingdom exhausted by centuries of bloodshed. Rather than continuing the cycle, he chose a different path. He refused to start new wars and instead focused on rebuilding his people and preserving peace.

Many viewed this decision as weakness.

Others believed the young king had simply lost the courage of his ancestors.

One of those who took notice was Velmora.

Curious about the sudden change, Velmora traveled to Bell's castle to meet the young ruler herself. Expecting to find an inexperienced king, she instead found someone who had learned from the mistakes of generations.

Bell explained that endless war had achieved nothing. Every victory had only created another enemy. Every king who sought glory had eventually fallen, leaving the next generation to continue the conflict.

During their conversation, Velmora jokingly suggested that if Bell hated pointless wars so much, he should unite the Overlords and make them stop fighting each other.

She laughed at the idea.

Bell did not.

What began as a joke quickly became a serious discussion. Together, the two Overlords developed a plan that many would have considered impossible: the creation of an alliance that could unite the Overlords under a common banner.

The task was enormous.

Velmora would seek out the strongest and most influential Overlords, while Bell would travel to negotiate with the rest.

Before they departed, Velmora gave Bell one final assignment.

Father Crow.

She knew she would never be able to convince him to join. Their history was far too complicated, and she understood that he would never trust her enough to listen.

Bell was far from eager to approach one of the most dangerous Overlords in existence. However, he also understood that an alliance without Father Crow would always be weaker than it could be.

Reluctantly, Bell accepted the responsibility.

And so, while Velmora sought out the strongest Overlords of the world, Bell began preparing for what would likely be the most difficult negotiation of his life.

AAfter developing the concept of the Overlord Alliance alongside Velmora, Bell helped draft the agreement that would serve as its foundation. Once the terms were completed, the two divided the task of recruitment between them. Velmora would approach some of the Overlords, while Bell would approach the rest.

Bell's greatest concern was Father Crow. While Velmora doubted she could convince him to join, Bell understood that an alliance without Father Crow would be significantly weaker.

Wanting to avoid a direct confrontation, Bell carefully planned his approach. He asked the Nature Overlord to remain nearby in case matters escalated and manipulated Queen Cruel into challenging Father Crow. Bell did not expect her to win. His goal was simply to draw Father Crow out personally.

The plan succeeded.

While Father Crow's attention was focused on Queen Cruel, Bell intervened and used gravity magic to pin him down. Bell had expected a difficult negotiation, but to his surprise Father Crow accepted the invitation almost immediately.

What Bell did not realize was that Father Crow valued strength and cunning above nearly everything else. By successfully drawing him out, catching him off guard, and forcing him to kneel, Bell had already earned his respect before the discussion had truly begun.

With Father Crow recruited, Bell continued his work and eventually succeeded in gathering the invited Overlords.

To host the first gathering, Bell personally prepared a meeting place designed to be as welcoming and neutral as possible. He constructed booths for each participant and made every effort to ensure the meeting would proceed peacefully.

One by one, the Overlords arrived.

For a time, everything appeared to be going exactly as planned.

That changed when Father Crow arrived.

His attempt to strike Velmora instantly shattered the calm atmosphere. Malqorath blocked the attack, but the gathering had already fallen into confusion. Across the meeting grounds, Overlords reached for their weapons and prepared themselves for whatever might happen next.

Bell watched as confusion spread through the gathering.

He had spent countless hours building the Alliance, only to see it appear on the verge of collapse before it had even been formed. More than Father Crow's attack itself, Bell feared the possibility that the Overlords would descend into open conflict.

Before the situation could deteriorate further, Charly appeared.

The sudden arrival of a Demon God immediately drew the attention of everyone present and shifted the focus away from the confrontation. Charly then presented a revised version of the agreement, offering terms that better suited the interests of the individual Overlords.

Bell disliked the idea of a Demon God becoming involved in the Alliance's foundation. However, Charly made it clear that he had no interest in leadership and sought only the souls promised through his agreements.

Bell remained hesitant, but the other Overlords accepted the revised terms.

Faced with the choice between preserving the Alliance or allowing it to fail, Bell reluctantly agreed.

At that moment, the completion of the Alliance mattered more to him than anything else.

As part of the Alliance's revised agreement, Charly introduced a series of tournaments between the Overlords. While he openly admitted that the tournaments existed largely so he could observe and evaluate the strength of the participants, Bell had no objection. His only request was to be allowed to enter late and fight Father Crow directly.

Charly accepted immediately.

Unlike most of the Alliance, Charly already knew Bell's true strength.

Bell had little interest in fighting the other participants. From the moment the tournament was announced, he intended to face Father Crow. Given Father Crow's reputation and abilities, Bell considered it almost certain that he would make it far enough for the two of them to meet.

As the tournament progressed, Bell watched Father Crow's battles with growing frustration. Long before the Alliance had been formed, Father Crow had earned a reputation for cruelty. He had killed four Overlords and left countless others suffering in his pursuit of strength. The tournament only reinforced that image. Opponents were not simply defeated. Many were left on the verge of death or burdened with injuries that would take years to fully heal.

Bell was especially angered by what happened to Orchestris.

After visiting Orchestris during his recovery, Bell entered the arena for his long-awaited match against Father Crow.

The reaction from the gathered Overlords was immediate. Most were confused by Bell's sudden appearance and expected Father Crow to win. Few among them knew the true extent of Bell's power.

As soon as the battle began, Bell used gravity magic to pin Father Crow down, just as he had during their first meeting.

The battle quickly became one-sided.

Bell advanced while Father Crow struggled merely to stand. Using his bells as weapons, Bell repeatedly struck him and hurled him across the arena. Every attempt to rise was met with crushing gravity and another barrage of attacks.

Each blow carried Bell's frustration toward the Overlord whose actions had caused so much suffering, both before and after the Alliance was formed.

For most of the fight, Father Crow was unable to fight back.

Eventually, after enduring the assault for far longer than anyone expected, Father Crow gathered every ounce of strength he had left and forced himself forward. Using the last of his stamina, he managed to land a single devastating blow on Bell.

The strike sent Bell flying across the arena.

For a brief moment, many believed Father Crow had turned the battle around.

Instead, Bell revealed his true form.

Transforming into the Nightmare Bell, he immediately overwhelmed the exhausted Father Crow and knocked him unconscious.

The attack was so violent that Charly was forced to intervene before Bell could kill him.

The damage inflicted during the battle was so severe that Father Crow could no longer continue competing in the tournament.

Bell, however, continued fighting and eventually claimed victory in the first tournament.

After the tournament concluded, Charly revealed the official standings. Bell expected to see Father Crow much lower on the list. Although Father Crow had been one of the strongest participants, he had been eliminated early and never had the opportunity to continue competing.

To Bell's surprise, Father Crow had been placed directly beneath him in second place.

When Bell questioned the ranking, Charly explained his reasoning.

In Charly's eyes, Father Crow had been denied the opportunity to finish the tournament properly. Had Bell not inflicted such severe injuries upon him, Charly was convinced Father Crow would have defeated the remaining participants and secured second place regardless.

At the same time, Charly revealed something else.

The tournament standings were not the same as his personal assessment of the Overlords' true strength.

Privately, Charly maintained a second list based entirely on his own observations. On that list, Velmora occupied the top position, followed by Malqorath, with Bell placed third.

Charly kept this ranking to himself.

Over the following tournaments, Charly's interest in Father Crow proved justified.

With each tournament, Father Crow adapted further to Bell's gravity magic. Techniques that had once completely restrained him became less effective, and even the Nightmare Bell gradually lost the overwhelming advantage it once possessed.

Eventually, Father Crow surpassed Bell.

Bell accepted this without resentment.

What concerned him was never the possibility of being surpassed. Bell knew there would always be Overlords capable of opposing Father Crow if necessary.

Instead, Bell worried about what might be lost if Father Crow ever turned against the Alliance.

Father Crow had become one of its most influential members. If he abandoned everything they had built, the damage would reach far beyond any battlefield.

To Bell's surprise, those fears never became reality.

Years passed.

The Alliance endured.

The meetings continued.

The tournaments continued.

Despite growing stronger with every passing year, Father Crow remained part of the Alliance, and the peace Bell had worked so hard to create remained intact.

Then came the war against Hell.

The war changed everything.

When it ended, the Overlords gradually drifted apart. The meetings became less frequent. The events that had once brought them together disappeared. The friendships and connections built over years of cooperation slowly faded.

Watching the Alliance decline was far more painful for Bell than watching Father Crow surpass him.

For years, he had dedicated himself to creating a future where the Overlords could stand together. Seeing that future slowly unravel left him exhausted and disheartened.

Eventually, Bell allowed himself to rest.

He withdrew from the world and entered a long slumber.

Only after Velmora restored the Alliance did Bell awaken and return to active life once more.
