# Lore

Bell was born into the royal family of the Bell Kingdom, one of the oldest Overlord bloodlines in existence. For generations, the Bell royalty had a single obsession: war.

The bells possessed exceptionally long lifespans, yet they spent those centuries fighting endless conflicts. Kings and queens marched to battle without hesitation, seeking glory, territory, and honor. Each generation inherited the grudges of the last, and the wars never truly ended.

Bell's parents were among the many rulers who died pursuing those ambitions.

Still young when he inherited the throne, Bell found himself ruling a kingdom exhausted by centuries of bloodshed. Rather than continuing the cycle, he chose a different path. He refused to start new wars and instead focused on rebuilding his people and preserving peace.

Many viewed this decision as weakness.

Others believed the young king had simply lost the courage of his ancestors.

One of those who took notice was Velmora.

Curious about the sudden change, Velmora traveled to Bell's castle to meet the young ruler herself. Expecting to find an inexperienced king, she instead found someone who had learned from the mistakes of generations.

Bell explained that endless war had achieved nothing. Every victory had only created another enemy. Every king who sought glory had eventually fallen, leaving the next generation to continue the conflict.

During their conversation, Velmora jokingly suggested that if Bell hated pointless wars so much, he should unite the Overlords and make them stop fighting each other.

She laughed at the idea.

Bell did not.

What began as a joke quickly became a serious discussion. Together, the two Overlords developed a plan that many would have considered impossible: the creation of an alliance that could unite the Overlords under a common banner.

The task was enormous.

Velmora would seek out the strongest and most influential Overlords, while Bell would travel to negotiate with the rest.

Before they departed, Velmora gave Bell one final assignment.

Father Crow.

She knew she would never be able to convince him to join. Their history was far too complicated, and she understood that he would never trust her enough to listen.

Bell was far from eager to approach one of the most dangerous Overlords in existence. However, he also understood that an alliance without Father Crow would always be weaker than it could be.

Reluctantly, Bell accepted the responsibility.

And so, while Velmora sought out the strongest Overlords of the world, Bell began preparing for what would likely be the most difficult negotiation of his life.
