# Urmeth

**Age:** Unknown    
**Size:** 3.50 m   
**Magic:** ice    
**Source of Magic:** the god of ice Glasivor 
---
# Lore
Urmeth was the Giant King of the North, forged by Glasivor, God of Ice, to stand as both shield and ruler of the frozen lands. From his creation, Urmeth was a warrior of rare virtue. He never ruled from behind a throne—when his people were threatened, he stood at the front lines, blade in hand, bearing the full weight of battle himself.

To sustain him through endless war, Glasivor gifted Urmeth a sacred sword—
a weapon capable of draining the energy of his enemies and channeling it back into its wielder, ensuring that Urmeth would never tire so long as battle continued. With this blade, Urmeth became unstoppable in defense, a living bulwark against all who threatened the North.

He was not only powerful, but kind. Urmeth united the fractured northern tribes and giant clans under a single banner, ending generations of conflict. Together, Urmeth and Glasivor sealed the North against evil, keeping abyssal creatures and demons far from its borders.

Then came the turning point.

Glasivor was forced to leave the North to confront the God of Chaos, entrusting its protection entirely to Urmeth. For the first time, the Giant King stood alone.

The enemy came without mercy.

Hundreds upon hundreds of abyssal creatures and demons poured into the North, attacking with relentless fury. Urmeth and his armies fought with everything they had, but they were outnumbered beyond hope. Seeing his people on the brink of annihilation, Urmeth made a final command—
his armies were ordered to fall back.

Urmeth remained.

In desperation, he unleashed the full power of his blade, no longer limiting what it consumed. The sword drained the energy of everything around him—enemy, battlefield, and even the abyss itself. The invasion was obliterated. The North was saved.

But the victory carried a terrible cost.

By drawing in abyssal power, Urmeth’s mind began to slowly corrupt. His body grew larger, his presence heavier, as if the cold itself bent around him. His eyes burned red, and time no longer seemed to touch him as it once had. Where there had been restraint, there was now dominance. Where there had been mercy, there was fear.

The hero became a tyrant.

Urmeth ruled the North with an iron fist, his sheer power enough to crush any who dared challenge him. None could stand against him—not giants, not demons, not mortals.

When Glasivor finally returned, he was shocked by what Urmeth had become.

Yet… he did not stop him.

So long as Urmeth remained loyal, so long as the North stood protected and chaos was kept at bay, Glasivor allowed the corruption to remain.

From that moment onward, Urmeth was no longer merely a king.

He became the Overlord of the North
