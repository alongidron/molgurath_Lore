# Velmora

**Age:** Unknown    
**Size:** 1.75      
**Magic:** everything     
**Source of Magic:** her witch's artifact and other witches
One of Velmora's greatest creations was the crystal ball she used to prevent her ascension into godhood. Rather than simply sealing away half of her power, Velmora converted that power into a vast reserve of mana stored within the artifact.

The crystal ball serves as an external source of magical energy. Whenever Velmora requires additional mana, she can draw upon the power stored within it, allowing her to sustain powerful spells for far longer than most magic users.

Velmora's strength also increases in the presence of other witches. Through her deep connection to witch magic, she can draw upon the magical energy naturally produced by nearby witches. The more witches that surround her, the more powerful she becomes, making her at her strongest when fighting alongside her people.
---
# Lore
Velmora is the daughter of the God of Purple Flame and Agatha, the Goddess of the Witches. She grew up alongside her sisters, Shyama and Haruka, and for a time, their family lived happily.

That peace ended when Velmora's father was corrupted by the Abyss. Agatha took her daughters and fled, trying to keep them safe, but they could not escape him forever.

Eventually, Agatha was forced to fight him. She emerged victorious, but was poisoned during the battle. Knowing she had little time left, she trained Velmora and her sisters in everything they would need to survive: magic, divine power, leadership, and the truth about the gods.

After Agatha's death, Velmora and her sisters waited for the day they would ascend and rule the Witch Kingdom as goddesses.

Life in the Area of Chaos was brutal, but the sisters protected their people from monsters, demons, gods, and countless other threats.

Then one day, Death appeared in their castle.

He asked the sisters to help end the chaos era.

Velmora refused.

She reminded Death that his and Life's actions had cost her both of her parents, and that the chaos era was a disaster created by the gods themselves.

As far as Velmora was concerned, they could solve their own problems.

After refusing Death's request, Velmora expected retaliation. She was certain that sooner or later Death would send assassins or divine punishment to deal with her.

Instead, a different threat appeared.

The Dark Mage, Malvek, arrived at the Witch Kingdom. He had not been sent by Death, nor was he acting on anyone's orders. Malvek believed the sisters were wasting their power by focusing only on protecting their kingdom while the rest of the world suffered through the chaos era. Deciding they were unworthy of their strength, he came to kill them himself.

Before the battle began, Malvek spoke of Overlords—beings of immense power who forged their own paths and bowed to no god or king.

The idea fascinated Velmora.

The battle that followed became one of the greatest challenges of the sisters' lives. Velmora, Shyama, and Haruka fought together with everything they had, yet no matter what they tried, they could not kill Malvek.

Realizing victory through force was impossible, the sisters combined their magic and sealed him away.

Although Malvek had come to kill them, the encounter left a lasting impression on Velmora. More than the battle itself, she became obsessed with the concept of Overlords. They possessed incredible power, freedom, and independence. Unlike gods, they were not bound by duties, expectations, or divine responsibilities.

From that day forward, Velmora no longer dreamed of becoming the goddess her mother wanted her to be.

She wanted to become an Overlord.

Years later, the Librarian arrived to guide the sisters toward ascension.

Velmora refused.

The Librarian warned her that she was too powerful to remain a mortal. Her destiny was to become a goddess.

Velmora rejected that destiny.

To prevent her ascension, she created her first witch artifact: a crystal ball capable of storing magical power. Into it she sealed half of her strength, reducing herself below the threshold required for godhood.

The Librarian was displeased, but he could not deny her determination. In the end, he agreed to let her remain mortal, though he left her with a warning.

If she ever reclaimed her full power, she would immediately become the goddess she was meant to be.

Velmora accepted without hesitation.

And so, for the first time in their lives, the three sisters went their separate ways.

Shyama and Haruka followed the path of godhood.

Velmora chose the path of the Overlord.

Over the years, Velmora continued her pursuit of magical knowledge. During this time, she created her daughter, Sylvara, and ruled the Witch Kingdom while constantly expanding her understanding of magic.

Eventually, her curiosity became impossible to ignore.

Ever since meeting Malvek, Velmora had been fascinated by Overlords. She wanted to meet every Overlord she could find, learn how they gained their power, and discover whether there were beings stronger than herself.

However, she refused to leave her people defenseless. Before departing, she placed the crown upon Sylvara's head and entrusted the kingdom to her daughter.

With that, Velmora began her journey.

For many years she traveled across countless lands, meeting kings, rulers, monsters, gods, and Overlords. Every encounter taught her something new. Combined with her natural talent for learning magic, her travels only increased her confidence.

As the years passed, Velmora began to believe there was very little left in the world that could truly threaten her.

She was wrong.

The first Overlord she came to regret meeting was the one known as Overlord Death.

Before meeting him, Velmora assumed the title was simply a nickname earned through reputation. She did not believe anyone could truly wield death magic itself.

When she finally found him, she realized how wrong she had been.

Everything around him was dead.

The land was dead.

The plants were dead.

Even the air felt lifeless.

Curious and confident in her abilities, Velmora challenged him.

The battle was unlike anything she had ever experienced. Every spell she cast was simply killed. Every magical construct she created perished before reaching its target. Anything she sent against him died.

For the first time in her life, Velmora faced a power she could not overcome.

That day she learned a lesson she would never forget.

Anyone capable of truly controlling death magic was among the most terrifying beings in existence.

Recognizing that continuing the fight would accomplish nothing, Velmora withdrew.

It was the first true draw of her life.

The second Overlord she came to regret meeting was Father Crow.

After sensing the disappearance of the Crow King, Velmora traveled to investigate. She expected answers.

Instead, she was attacked the moment she arrived.

To Velmora's surprise, she enjoyed the battle. The fight awakened the murderous instincts she normally kept buried. For a time, she fully intended to kill Father Crow.

Before she could unleash the final blow, Osricain intervened and brought the battle to an end.

As she prepared to leave, Father Crow gave her a warning.

"You will regret not killing me."

Velmora dismissed the threat.

She believed she could stop him whenever she wished.

She was wrong.

Years later, as news of Father Crow's conquests, massacres, and growing influence spread across the world, Velmora found herself wondering whether she could still defeat him at all. More importantly, she wondered how many lives might have been saved if Osricain had not stopped her that day.

One day, while traveling, she noticed something unusual.

The lands surrounding Bell's territory felt peaceful.

Curious, she went to visit Bell and discovered his ambitious goal: uniting the Overlords of Grymmwald beneath a single banner.

Velmora thought the idea was impossible.

Bell believed otherwise.

Although she doubted the alliance would succeed, Velmora also understood the danger of allowing powerful Overlords such as Father Crow to act without restraint. For the first time, she began to see value in Bell's dream.

Together, they began developing a plan that would eventually become the Overlord Alliance.

Velmora agreed to help recruit some of the most powerful and dangerous Overlords, but she warned Bell about one individual.

Father Crow.

She doubted anyone could convince him to cooperate.

When the day of the gathering finally arrived, Velmora's fears proved justified.

The moment Father Crow saw her, he attacked.

Fortunately, she reacted quickly enough to pull the Desert King into the path of the attack, preventing disaster.

Before the situation could escalate further, Charly appeared and forced the battle to stop.

Velmora disliked the arrangement that followed. She did not trust demons, and she trusted demon gods even less. However, she wanted the alliance to succeed, and Charly's proposal offered the best chance of making that happen.

Reluctantly, she agreed.

To settle their unfinished rivalry, Velmora and Father Crow were transported to an arena while Charly and the gathered Overlords watched.

Velmora fought with everything she had.

In the end, Father Crow emerged victorious.

Despite her defeat, the alliance was successfully formed.

With her duties complete, Velmora returned to the Witch Kingdom.

There she discovered that Sylvara was no longer queen.

The current ruler explained what had happened. Sylvara had abandoned the throne and disappeared long ago.

Velmora was disappointed. Her daughter had not even managed to hold the crown for a full year before walking away from it.

As she prepared to introduce herself to the new queen, an old enemy returned.

Malvek.

Yet something was different.

He seemed weaker than before.

During the battle, Velmora noticed the source of the change: a crystal that was sustaining him. Working alongside the new queen, she destroyed the crystal and finally helped bring Malvek down.

After witnessing the new queen's power firsthand, Velmora reached a decision.

The kingdom no longer needed her on its throne.

The new queen had proven herself worthy of the crown.

So Velmora allowed her to remain queen while she continued handling matters beyond the kingdom's borders—particularly those involving Overlords.
